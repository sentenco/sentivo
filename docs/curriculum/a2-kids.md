# A2 Kids Curriculum

All 12 units of the A2 Kids track (ages 6–12), each broken down lesson by
lesson. Continues directly after the completed A1 Kids course. Every
lesson is still 25 minutes, but uses a different 6-stage shape than A1 —
A2 kids are ready for short texts, sequencing, guided writing, and
mini-projects, not just vocabulary-plus-drill: **Warm-up/recycle (3 min)
→ Language in context (5 min) → Short text task: reading/listening/
picture-text (7 min) → Guided speaking or writing (6 min) → Personalized
task (2 min) → Review/mini-project (2 min)**.

This is content/planning-stage only — none of it is built into the app
yet (no Supabase rows, no slide-type mapping, no images/audio).

Unit topics were proposed by Perplexity (not fixed in advance, unlike
A1) to continue logically from A1 Kids without repeating it. Checked
against the A1 Kids topic list (Hello and My Name, My Day, My Family,
Colors and Numbers, Classroom Things, Animals, My Toys, Food I Like, My
Body, Weather Today, My House, My Feelings) — no direct repeats, each
unit meaningfully extends a related A1 area or introduces a new
real-world topic appropriate for A2. Grammar targets (simple past,
comparatives, connectors and/but/because, "going to" future saved for
the final unit) match the A1→A2 jump.

One thing worth being aware of, not fixed: Unit 2 (Hobbies) and Unit 6
(Sports) share light vocabulary overlap ("skate"/"ride a bike" vs.
"skating"/"cycling") — reads as reasonable spaced repetition rather than
redundancy, since sports are a subset of hobbies and the two units
differentiate otherwise (general preferences + simple past vs. organized
sports + comparatives + a past-tense sports-day story).

## Proposed Unit Topics

1. School Life and School Projects
2. Hobbies and Free Time
3. Clothes and Getting Ready
4. Places in Town
5. Shopping and Money
6. Sports and Outdoor Fun
7. Friends and Getting Along
8. Food, Cooking, and Healthy Habits
9. Trips, Holidays, and Travel
10. Stories and Imagination
11. Festivals and Special Days
12. Helping at Home and Future Plans

# Unit 1: School Life and School Projects

## Lesson 1: School Subjects

| Part | Plan |
|------|------|
| Time | 25 minutes |
| Main aim | The student can say which school subjects they have and give simple opinions about them. |
| Materials | Subject picture slides, timetable spinner, sentence frame cards, mini opinion poll |

| Stage | Time | Online material / activity | Teacher role |
|-------|------|----------------------------|--------------|
| Warm-up (recycle) | 3 min | Quick picture race reviewing A1 classroom things, then linking them to new school subject icons | Recycle familiar school language and bridge it to the new unit topic |
| Language in context | 5 min | Interactive timetable slide introducing subject words with simple opinions: I like art because it is fun. | Model the new subject vocabulary and the connector because in short kid-friendly examples |
| Short text task (reading/listening/picture-text) | 7 min | Picture-text school timetable with 4 short sentences about Ben’s Monday; student matches each sentence to a subject block | Guide reading for gist and detail, highlighting subject names and simple opinion language |
| Guided speaking or writing | 6 min | Speaking ladder: the student says 3 sentences about their timetable using a prompt strip | Prompt full short sentences and support pronunciation and word order |
| Personalized task | 2 min | Student chooses a favorite subject emoji and completes: My favorite subject is ___ because ___. | Encourage a personal response with light correction |
| Review / mini-project | 2 min | Tiny digital timetable card with one sentence caption | Praise success and recap the new subject words |
