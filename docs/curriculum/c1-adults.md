# Adults C1 Curriculum

All six domains of the Adults C1 track, each a 9-lesson unit built from three
3-lesson mode blocks (Performance, Model, Output). This uses the C1/C2
framework's communicative-control model rather than the fixed 6-stage/
25-minute template used for A1-B2 — see the C1/C2 framework memory for the
full rationale (output-upgrade loop, ESL Pragmatic and Nuance Scorecard,
Speaking Simulator, advanced grammar as stylistic toolkit).

Each unit uses only the five canonical Speaking Simulator patterns (frozen
vocabulary across Adults C1, Adults C2, and Teens C1): Timed Opinion Turn,
Rapid Reformulation, Challenge & Pushback, Negotiated Decision, Mediation
Brief.

Units are numbered by domain-list position (Unit N = domain #N), consistent
with the six-unit mode-rotation table:

| Unit | Domain | Mode order |
|---|---|---|
| 1 | Argument and Disagreement | Performance → Model → Output |
| 2 | Mediation and Synthesis | Model → Output → Performance |
| 3 | Professional and Formal Writing | Output → Performance → Model |
| 4 | Presentation and Briefing | Performance → Model → Output |
| 5 | Problem-Solving and Negotiation | Model → Output → Performance |
| 6 | Register, Nuance, and Diplomatic Control | Output → Performance → Model |

**Correction made before consolidation:** Units 2 and 5 were originally
drafted using the default Performance→Model→Output order (the same bug
caught and fixed for Units 3 and 6 earlier), rather than their correct
Model→Output→Performance rotation slot. Fixed here via the same
content-preserving cyclic re-sequencing used for Units 3/6 — lesson content
unchanged, only reordered to match the validated rotation.

This is content/planning-stage only — none of it is built into the app yet
(no Supabase rows, no slide-type mapping, no images/audio).

# Unit 1: Argument and Disagreement

### Mode order: Performance → Model → Output

| Lesson | Dominant mode | Simulator patterns | Framing |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Performance | Timed Opinion Turn + light Rapid Reformulation | Learners take quick positions on controversial but low-stakes work or public issues; immediate upgrades focus on clearer stance and reasons. |
| 2 | Performance | Challenge & Pushback | Teacher or partner challenges learner opinions; learners practise holding ground, softening, or refining claims. |
| 3 | Performance | Negotiated Decision (short scenario) | Small decision tasks with conflicting priorities, such as choosing between two policies or actions where values clash. |
| 4 | Model | Timed Opinion Turn (light, around model input) | Analyse a strong opinion piece or short talk to notice how arguments are opened, structured, and closed, then give shorter framed opinion turns. |
| 5 | Model | Mediation Brief (guided, 2 short sources) | Compare two contrasting viewpoints or articles on the same issue and practise summarising both fairly before showing where they differ. |
| 6 | Model | Negotiated Decision (guided model analysis) | Study a model disagreement or decision dialogue and map how speakers propose, concede, and reframe. |
| 7 | Output | Mediation Brief → written mediation/recommendation note | Learners write a short mediation note explaining two sides of a dispute and recommending a balanced course of action. |
| 8 | Output | Negotiated Decision → written recommendation/decision summary | Learners write a recommendation or decision summary for a third party, justifying the chosen option and acknowledging trade-offs. |
| 9 | Output | Timed Opinion Turn → structured opinion/reflection piece | Learners turn a spoken stance on a recurring disagreement into a structured opinion or reflection text. |

# Unit 2: Mediation and Synthesis

### Mode order: Model → Output → Performance

| Lesson | Dominant mode | Simulator patterns | Framing |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Model | Timed Opinion Turn (light, around model input) | Analyse a strong “mediator’s voice” sample that frames both sides; learners then do short, framed opinion turns that emphasise balance rather than advocacy. |
| 2 | Model | Mediation Brief (guided, 2 short sources) | Compare two short texts representing different sides of a disagreement and practise summarising each fairly before linking or contrasting them. |
| 3 | Model | Negotiated Decision (guided model analysis) | Study a model mediation or decision conversation and map how the mediator summarises, checks understanding, and proposes options. |
| 4 | Output | Mediation Brief → written mediation/recommendation note | Learners write a mediation brief that explains both sides for a decision-maker and recommends a next step, with careful attribution and neutral tone. |
| 5 | Output | Negotiated Decision → written recommendation/decision summary | Learners write a decision summary that records the compromise, the reasoning, and any conditions, aimed at stakeholders who were not in the room. |
| 6 | Output | Timed Opinion Turn → structured stance/reflection piece | Learners write a reflective or advisory piece on how to handle conflicts between groups or viewpoints, showing synthesis of recurring patterns rather than one case only. |
| 7 | Performance | Timed Opinion Turn + light Rapid Reformulation | Learners give quick personal takes on complex multi-stakeholder situations and refine how neutrally and clearly they frame the situation. |
| 8 | Performance | Challenge & Pushback | Learners propose how to handle a conflict and are challenged from each side’s perspective; they practise clarifying, reframing, and avoiding taking sides too early. |
| 9 | Performance | Negotiated Decision (short scenario) | Short mediation-style decisions where the learner must articulate a middle ground under time pressure. |

# Unit 3: Professional and Formal Writing

### Mode order: Output → Performance → Model

| Lesson | Dominant mode | Simulator patterns | Framing |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Output | Mediation Brief → written synthesis/recommendation note | Learners produce a short written synthesis or recommendation note that integrates multiple sources or viewpoints clearly and accurately. |
| 2 | Output | Negotiated Decision → written proposal/report section | Learners write a proposal section, recommendation, or report paragraph that sets out options, evaluates them, and argues for a preferred course of action. |
| 3 | Output | Timed Opinion Turn → structured formal writing task | Learners turn a spoken stance into a more polished written product such as an opinion paragraph, short formal response, executive summary, or reflective academic note. |
| 4 | Performance | Timed Opinion Turn + light Rapid Reformulation | Learners give quick spoken positions on practical or academic prompts, then tighten phrasing so the spoken stance can feed later writing. |
| 5 | Performance | Challenge & Pushback | Learners present a claim, recommendation, or interpretation and respond to sceptical questions that force clearer justification and more careful qualification. |
| 6 | Performance | Negotiated Decision (short scenario) | Learners discuss options or interpretations and work toward a decision that later becomes the basis for a written recommendation, proposal, or summary. |
| 7 | Model | Timed Opinion Turn (light, around model input) | Analyse a model report paragraph, abstract, recommendation note, or formal email to notice how argument and stance are compressed in strong written discourse, then respond in shorter framed turns. |
| 8 | Model | Mediation Brief (guided, 2 short sources) | Compare two short readings, data points, or positions and practise verbally synthesising them before converting that synthesis into writing. |
| 9 | Model | Negotiated Decision (guided model analysis) | Study how a model written recommendation or proposal balances options, caveats, and rationale, then rehearse the logic orally before writing. |

# Unit 4: Presentation and Briefing

### Mode order: Performance → Model → Output

| Lesson | Dominant mode | Simulator patterns | Framing |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Performance | Timed Opinion Turn + light Rapid Reformulation | Learners give short impromptu mini-briefings (e.g. update a manager on X in 60–90 seconds) and immediately upgrade for clarity, focus, and audience relevance. |
| 2 | Performance | Challenge & Pushback | Learners present a short update or recommendation and handle questions or objections from a manager or client who wants clarification, more evidence, or a different priority. |
| 3 | Performance | Negotiated Decision (short scenario) | Learners present 2–3 options and, through brief discussion, steer toward a decision while keeping the briefing concise, structured, and useful. |
| 4 | Model | Timed Opinion Turn (light, around model input) | Analyse a strong briefing or short talk for structure, signposting, and emphasis, then deliver lighter framed updates that reuse those briefing moves. |
| 5 | Model | Mediation Brief (guided, 2 short sources) | Work with two short updates or reports and practise briefing a third party on both, surfacing what matters most for that audience. |
| 6 | Model | Negotiated Decision (guided model analysis) | Study a model meeting or briefing where someone presents options and guides a group toward a decision; map how they structure, prioritise, and answer follow-up questions. |
| 7 | Output | Mediation Brief → written/slide brief | Learners produce a written brief or slide outline that summarises a situation or project for a specific audience, using clear sections and signposting. |
| 8 | Output | Negotiated Decision → written recommendation/briefing note | Learners write a recommendation or decision briefing that sets context, presents options, and justifies the chosen path. |
| 9 | Output | Timed Opinion Turn → structured presentation/speaking notes | Learners convert a spoken mini-briefing into a short structured presentation plan or speaking notes for a longer talk. |

# Unit 5: Problem-Solving and Negotiation

### Mode order: Model → Output → Performance

| Lesson | Dominant mode | Simulator patterns | Framing |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Model | Timed Opinion Turn (light, around model input) | Analyse a model problem-solving discussion or management commentary to notice how problems and options are framed, then give lighter opinion turns reusing those frames. |
| 2 | Model | Mediation Brief (guided, 2 short sources) | Use two short problem descriptions from different stakeholders and practise summarising them for a decision-maker who must see the full picture. |
| 3 | Model | Negotiated Decision (guided model analysis) | Study a model negotiation to identify how proposals, concessions, and conditional offers are sequenced and phrased. |
| 4 | Output | Mediation Brief → written problem summary/options note | Learners write a structured problem brief outlining the situation, key constraints, and possible options, in a form a manager or client could read. |
| 5 | Output | Negotiated Decision → written recommendation/proposal | Learners write a proposal or recommendation document that presents the preferred solution, supporting arguments, and contingency plans. |
| 6 | Output | Timed Opinion Turn → structured argument/decision rationale | Learners turn a spoken defence of a chosen solution into a structured rationale suitable for email or a slide outline. |
| 7 | Performance | Timed Opinion Turn + light Rapid Reformulation | Learners take quick positions on how to handle practical problems and refine for clarity and feasibility. |
| 8 | Performance | Challenge & Pushback | Learners propose solutions and handle pushback from a stakeholder who questions cost, risk, or fairness; they practise justifying and adapting offers. |
| 9 | Performance | Negotiated Decision (short scenario) | Short, high-pressure negotiation tasks with clear but conflicting priorities. |

# Unit 6: Register, Nuance, and Diplomatic Control

### Mode order: Output → Performance → Model

| Lesson | Dominant mode | Simulator patterns | Framing |
|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | Output | Mediation Brief → written tone/register note | Learners write a short note or commentary explaining how two messages differ in stance, tone, or register and what would be more appropriate for a target audience. |
| 2 | Output | Negotiated Decision → written rewrite/recommendation | Learners rewrite or draft a message so that a decision, refusal, or condition is phrased with more tact, precision, and audience control. |
| 3 | Output | Timed Opinion Turn → structured reflection/register shift task | Learners turn a spoken response into a structured piece that shows deliberate register shifting, such as formalising an informal point or softening an overly direct one. |
| 4 | Performance | Timed Opinion Turn + light Rapid Reformulation | Learners respond quickly to sensitive prompts, then immediately reformulate to adjust strength, tact, certainty, or interpersonal tone. |
| 5 | Performance | Challenge & Pushback | Learners defend or soften a position under pressure, practising hedging, diplomatic disagreement, face-saving, and recalibration of tone when challenged. |
| 6 | Performance | Negotiated Decision (short scenario) | Learners work through a short decision task where the main challenge is not the content itself but how to phrase conditions, disagreement, and compromise appropriately. |
| 7 | Model | Timed Opinion Turn (light, around model input) | Analyse model examples for stance marking, hedging, tact, emphasis, understatement, and shifts in formality, then give short framed responses that reuse those moves. |
| 8 | Model | Mediation Brief (guided, 2 short sources) | Compare two texts or voices with different tones or implied stances and practise briefing a third party on how they differ not only in content but in register and interpersonal effect. |
| 9 | Model | Negotiated Decision (guided model analysis) | Study a model interaction to notice how speakers soften claims, signal caution, disagree tactfully, and reframe sensitive points without sounding vague or weak. |
