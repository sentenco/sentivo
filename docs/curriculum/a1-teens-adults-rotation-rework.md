# A1 Teens & Adults — Task-Format Rotation Rework (proposal, not yet applied)

**Status: for review. No lesson content or SQL has been changed yet —
this file only proposes the rotation pattern.**

## The problem this fixes

Right now, every unit in A1 Teens and A1 Adults uses the exact same
task-format-to-lesson-position mapping:

| Lesson | Format (currently, every single unit) |
|---|---|
| L1 | Matching / notice |
| L2 | Fill-in-the-blank |
| L3 | Error-spot / role reversal |
| L4 | Role-play / production |
| L5 | Mixed review |

Within one unit this feels varied. Across multiple units it doesn't —
by Unit 3 or 4, "L1 is always matching, L4 is always role-play" becomes
a fully predictable rule, which recreates the same flatness problem the
whole progression framework was built to solve, just moved up a level.

## The fix

Keep the **difficulty arc** fixed to lesson position (L1 stays the
lightest/most scaffolded lesson in a unit, L4 stays the heaviest/most
independent one, L5 stays review) — that escalation is what makes the
lesson-level progression legible, and it's not the thing causing
flatness.

Vary the **task format** that fills each position, unit to unit, drawn
from the same 4-format library already established for each track:

- **M** — Matching / notice (teacher models, student echoes)
- **F** — Fill-in-the-blank with a word bank
- **E** — Error-spot / choose-the-better-response (role reversal)
- **R** — Role-play / mini production task

The table below is a balanced rotation: each format appears in each
lesson position exactly 3 times across the 12 units, so there's no
"L1 is always X" rule to notice, but it's not random either — no two
units share the same order, and the distribution stays even so no
format quietly dominates one position.

## Rotation matrix (applies to both Teens and Adults — same positions, each track keeps its own existing format *flavor*)

| Unit | L1 | L2 | L3 | L4 | L5 |
|------|----|----|----|----|----|
| 1  | M | F | E | R | Review |
| 2  | F | R | M | E | Review |
| 3  | E | M | R | F | Review |
| 4  | R | E | F | M | Review |
| 5  | M | R | F | E | Review |
| 6  | F | E | R | M | Review |
| 7  | R | M | E | F | Review |
| 8  | E | F | M | R | Review |
| 9  | F | M | E | R | Review |
| 10 | R | F | M | E | Review |
| 11 | M | E | R | F | Review |
| 12 | E | R | F | M | Review |

Unit 1 keeps the original order (M-F-E-R) as the anchor everyone
recognizes; every other unit reshuffles from there.

## What "format flavor" means per track (unchanged from what's already built)

The letters above are the same across Teens and Adults, but what each
letter actually *looks like* stays track-appropriate, matching what's
already in the committed content:

| Format | Teens flavor | Adults flavor |
|---|---|---|
| **M** Matching | Teacher models a line, student echoes it | Student reads a short model text, identifies the target phrase |
| **F** Fill-in-blank | Guided Q&A exchange with a word bank | Same, functional register (forms/messages) |
| **E** Error-spot | Student asks first, teacher answers (role reversal) | Choose-the-better-response between two model answers |
| **R** Role-play | Mini role-play combining the unit's language | Realistic transaction role-play (shop, workplace, appointment) |

## Worked example: what changes in practice

**Unit 5 (Teens, "Hobbies and Interests"), current L1** uses the
Matching format:

> Activity: *"My hobby is [football]." / "I like [dancing]." / "I like
> [painting] too."* — teacher models, student echoes.

**Under the new matrix, Unit 5's L1 becomes Format M still** (the
matrix above happens to keep M at L1 for Unit 5 too) — so no change
there. But **Unit 5's L2 shifts from F to R** (role-play instead of
fill-in-blank), meaning what's currently a controlled fill-in-the-blank
lesson would become a short guided role-play instead, while staying at
L2's difficulty level (still more scaffolded than L4's role-play would
be — shorter dialogue, more of the language pre-supplied).

I have **not** rewritten any lesson content yet. Once this matrix is
approved, the next step is applying it lesson-by-lesson across all 12
units for Teens, then Adults, rewriting each lesson's Activity and
Speaking slides to match its new assigned format while keeping the
vocabulary/reading/writing content already written (which doesn't need
to change — only the *shape* of the practice task does).
