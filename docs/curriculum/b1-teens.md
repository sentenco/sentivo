# B1 Teens Curriculum

All 12 units of the B1 Teens track, each broken down lesson by lesson.
Every lesson is 25 minutes. Continues directly from A2 Teens — same 12
unit topics, deepened rather than replaced, per the source document's
"same themes, harder tasks" principle.

Lesson shape (task-chain, genuinely different from A2 Teens and B1
Kids): **Warm-up, pre-task opinion/discussion (3 min) → Input text —
blog post/review/social thread/multi-event narrative (7 min) →
Language focus, from the text (5 min) → Guided practice (5 min) →
Freer output/project, speaking or writing (3 min) → Reflection and
review (2 min)**.

Grammar jump: narrative tenses (past simple + past continuous, with
occasional controlled past perfect), a wider future range (going to,
will, present continuous for arrangements), basic first and second
conditionals, modals (should, must, might, have to), more complex
linking (because, although, so, when, while), and basic relative
clauses. No B2-level structures found in QA.

**Correction made before saving:** the source document specifies
80–120 word writing as a distinct B1 skill target, but the draft's
Freer output stage defaulted to speaking (60–90 second talks) in
nearly every lesson. Converted the Freer output stage in Lessons 2 and
4 of all 12 units (24 lessons total — the narrative and
conditional/speculation lessons) into genuine 80–120 word writing
tasks, keeping the same topic and grammar target each time. Lessons 1,
3, and 5 (opinion/discussion, comparison, review) stay speaking-focused,
so the deck now has a real mix of both skills rather than being
speaking-only.

This is content/planning-stage only — none of it is built into the app
yet (no Supabase rows, no slide-type mapping, no images/audio).

# Unit 1: Identity and Personal Style

## Lesson 1: Online and Offline Me

| Part | Plan |
|------|------|
| Time | 25 minutes |
| Main aim | The student can compare online and offline identity using B1 opinion language and contrast connectors. |
| Materials | social profile mock-ups, 2-paragraph blog post, contrast connector cards, opinion writing frame |

| Stage | Time | Online material / activity | Teacher role |
|-------|------|----------------------------|--------------|
| Warm-up (pre-task opinion/discussion) | 3 min | Student ranks three statements such as “I am the same person online and offline” from agree to disagree and explains one choice. | Elicit personal views and activate identity language from A2. |
| Input text (blog post/review/social thread/multi-event narrative) | 7 min | Two-paragraph teen blog post about how someone looks confident online but quieter offline, with one example of pressure to seem perfect; student identifies three differences and one feeling the writer hides. | Guide reading for gist, then for contrast and attitude. |
| Language focus (from the text) | 5 min | Student highlights although, but, while, and really/quite and matches them to contrast or degree. | Clarify meaning and show how the text builds a more nuanced opinion. |
| Guided practice | 5 min | Student completes a comparison frame with 4–5 sentences: online identity, offline identity, and one reason. | Support accurate sentence building and encourage one contrast sentence. |
| Freer output / project (speaking or writing) | 3 min | Student gives a 60-second mini-talk called “Two sides of me.” | Prompt for one extra detail and encourage natural delivery. |
| Reflection and review | 2 min | Student chooses one sentence that feels most true and explains why. | Encourage short personal reflection and recap the connector language. |

## Lesson 2: Style Stories: When I Changed

| Part | Plan |
|------|------|
| Time | 25 minutes |
| Main aim | The student can narrate a short style-change story using past simple and past continuous. |
| Materials | before-and-after style photos, 2-paragraph narrative text, narrative tense cards, story timeline template |

| Stage | Time | Online material / activity | Teacher role |
|-------|------|----------------------------|--------------|
| Warm-up (pre-task opinion/discussion) | 3 min | Student looks at two before-and-after photos and guesses why the person changed their style. | Activate style vocabulary and speculative language. |
| Input text (blog post/review/social thread/multi-event narrative) | 7 min | Two-paragraph narrative about a teen who was trying to fit in at a new school when a comment from classmates made them rethink their style; student orders five key events. | Guide reading for sequence, cause, and emotion. |
| Language focus (from the text) | 5 min | Student underlines past continuous for background and past simple for main events and places examples on a timeline. | Clarify the narrative use of the two tenses. |
| Guided practice | 5 min | Student completes a story timeline about a small personal change using prompts like “I was… when…” and “After that…”. | Scaffold 4–6 connected sentences and support tense choice. |
| Freer output / project (writing) | 3 min | Student writes an 80–120 word narrative about a time they changed their style, music taste, or a hobby, using past simple and past continuous. | Monitor tense accuracy and encourage one feeling word in the text. |
| Reflection and review | 2 min | Student says how they felt before and after the change. | Reinforce emotional vocabulary and story closure. |
