# A1 Adults Curriculum

All 12 units of the A1 Adults track, each broken down lesson by lesson.
Every lesson is 25 minutes, built for online 1:1 teaching, with the same
6-stage shape used across all Sentivo tracks: **Warm-up (3 min) →
Presentation (5 min) → Guided practice (7 min) → Freer practice (6 min) →
Review (2 min) → Wrap-up (2 min)**.

This is content/planning-stage only — none of it is built into the app
yet (no Supabase rows, no slide-type mapping, no images/audio).

**Reordering note:** the Perplexity draft this was sourced from didn't
follow our finalized Adults roadmap topic order past Unit 3 — Units 4/5
(Job/Daily Routine) were swapped, Units 8/9/10 (Health, Travel, My City)
were shuffled, and the roadmap's "Time and Days" topic was dropped
(its content is already folded into the Daily Routine unit's Lesson 2).
In its place, Perplexity had generated a full "Technology and
Communication" unit that isn't part of the original roadmap at all. Since
a real content gap (Time and Days) and a real content surplus
(Technology and Communication) both existed, the two were paired up —
Unit 8 is now Technology and Communication instead of Time and Days. This
mirrors the same kind of swap already done for A1 Kids Units 11–12, and
gives Adults parity with the Teens track, which has its own Social
Media/Tech unit. Everything below is renumbered and reordered to this
final sequence; no lesson content was rewritten, only relabeled.

Content double-checked for A1-level and adult-appropriate register:
vocabulary and grammar stay within A1 (present simple throughout, no past
tense, no complex clauses) with two intentional exceptions already
established for Teens — "I'm going to..." (Unit 12) is a fixed plan
phrase, not taught grammar. Family, work, health, and technology
vocabulary are all adult-register (husband/wife/partner, not mom/dad;
office/meeting, not homework; email/join a meeting, not social apps),
correctly differentiated from the Kids and Teens versions of similar
topics.

# Unit 1: Greetings and Personal Information

## Lesson 1: Hello and Introductions

| Part | Plan |
|------|------|
| Time | 25 minutes |
| Main aim | The student can introduce themself and exchange basic personal information. |
| Materials | Slide deck, name card activity, text chat prompts, mini quiz slides |

| Stage | Time | Online material / activity | Teacher role |
|-------|------|----------------------------|--------------|
| Warm-up | 3 min | Quick hello routine plus on-screen name prompt | Build comfort, check mood, start speaking immediately |
| Presentation | 5 min | Simple greeting phrases, name, country, job model slides | Model language, highlight key phrase forms |
| Guided practice | 7 min | Drag-and-match: question and answer pairs for introductions | Correct answers, prompt repetition, support pronunciation |
| Freer practice | 6 min | Short live introduction exchange using personal details | Ask follow-up questions, encourage full simple answers |
| Review | 2 min | Quick recall slide with 3 target phrases | Confirm memory, recycle key language |
| Wrap-up | 2 min | Exit line: "Tell me your name and job." | End on success, give brief praise |
