# B1 Kids Curriculum

All 12 units of the B1 Kids track, each broken down lesson by lesson.
Every lesson is 25 minutes. Continues directly from A2 Kids — same 12
unit topics, deepened rather than replaced (matching the source
document's "same themes, harder tasks" principle for the A2→B1 jump).

Lesson shape (genuinely different from A2 Kids, more text/output than
before, still age-appropriate): **Warm-up (opinion/recycle) (3 min) →
Short text task, 2 short paragraphs (6 min) → Language noticing, from
the text (5 min) → Guided speaking or writing (7 min) → Personalized
task (2 min) → Review/mini-project (2 min)**.

Grammar jump: past simple + past continuous together for storytelling,
simple modals (can/could/should/have to) for ability/advice/obligation,
comparatives and superlatives, a very simple first conditional, basic
connectors (because/so/when/while), and very simple relative clauses
("the girl who..."). No passive voice, no second conditional, nothing
at B2 level — this was checked and holds throughout.

Output targets grew from A2: guided writing is 4–6 connected sentences
(up from A2's 3–4 lines), speaking turns up to 30–45 seconds.

This is content/planning-stage only — none of it is built into the app
yet (no Supabase rows, no slide-type mapping, no images/audio). QA
found no structural issues this time (the explicit stage-order
instruction in the prompt worked), no topic drift from A2, and no
content duplication.

# Unit 1: School Life and School Projects

## Lesson 1: My Busy School Morning

| Part | Plan |
|------|------|
| Time | 25 minutes |
| Main aim | The student can describe a school morning with connected actions and use because and so to explain simple choices. |
| Materials | school picture board, 2-paragraph school diary text, sequence cards, writing frame |

| Stage | Time | Online material / activity | Teacher role |
|-------|------|----------------------------|--------------|
| Warm-up (opinion/recycle) | 3 min | Student chooses the best and hardest part of a school morning from picture cards and says why. | Recycle A2 school routine language and prompt a longer opinion. |
| Short text task (a short story or article, 2 short paragraphs) | 6 min | Two short paragraphs from a child’s school diary about arriving late, forgetting a notebook, and solving the problem with a friend; student orders 4 event cards. | Guide reading for sequence and gist using visual support. |
| Language noticing (from the text) | 5 min | Student highlights because and so and matches each connector to a reason or result from the diary. | Help the learner notice how connected ideas are built in a short text. |
| Guided speaking or writing | 7 min | Student completes a 5-sentence writing frame about their own school morning using first, then, because, and so. | Model one sentence and support the learner in linking ideas clearly. |
| Personalized task | 2 min | Student says one thing that makes their morning easier. | Prompt a short connected personal response. |
| Review / mini-project | 2 min | Student creates a mini ‘good school morning’ checklist with 3 picture labels. | Review key routine language and end with a visual takeaway. |

| Personalized task | 2 min | Student says one small school problem they had before. | Encourage a short personal story using a past verb. |
| Review / mini-project | 2 min | Student records a one-line comic caption for the class project story. | Check control of the target story pattern in a playful way. |
