# A1 Teens — Intra-Level Progression Matrix (Pilot #2)

Per the Sentivo Intra-Level Progression Framework, plus the two follow-up
considerations: lesson-level escalation within a unit, and task-format
rotation per the 25-minute 1:1 lesson micro-pattern doc.

**Ceiling guardrail:** A1's grammar ceiling is well below A2's. Unlike
the A2 Teens pilot, A1's Bridge tier must NOT introduce true comparative
morphology (-er/more...than), simple past narrative, or "going to"
future — those are reserved for the A1→A2 jump. Where A1 needs to sound
a little more forward-looking or preference-based at the Bridge tier,
it uses lexical chunks already common at A1 ("I like it better", "I want
to...") rather than new productive grammar.

## Unit-level tiers

| Unit | Tier | Topic | Grammar load | Text length target | Vocabulary type | Scaffolding | Cognitive demand |
|------|------|-------|---------------|---------------------|------------------|--------------|-------------------|
| 1 | Foundation | Hello and Me | simple present ("I am / I have"), basic Wh-questions | 2-4 word clauses, 3-4 sentence texts | concrete, everyday | heavy: word banks, full frames | name / identify |
| 2 | Foundation | My Family | simple present + possessives (my/your/his/her) | 3-4 sentence texts | concrete (family nouns) | heavy | name / identify |
| 3 | Foundation | School | simple present + basic question forms (Do you...?) | 3-4 sentence texts | concrete (school nouns) | heavy | name / identify |
| 4 | Foundation | Friends and Free Time | simple present + simple negative (I don't like) | 3-4 sentence texts | concrete | heavy | name / identify |
| 5 | Combination | Hobbies and Interests | simple present + negative + short question, same exchange | 4-6 sentence texts | concrete + like/love/hate/favorite | medium: partial prompts | describe + give a simple preference |
| 6 | Combination | Daily Routine | simple present + frequency words (usually, always) | 4-6 sentence texts | concrete + routine words | medium | describe + sequence with time words |
| 7 | Combination | My Room | simple present + there is/are + prepositions of place | 4-6 sentence texts | concrete (room/furniture nouns) | medium | describe a place |
| 8 | Combination | Food and Drinks | simple present + like/don't like + simple question exchange | 4-6 sentence texts | concrete + simple opinion words | medium | describe + give a simple opinion |
| 9 | Bridge | Clothes and Style | simple present + "and"/"but" connector, combined in one sentence | 6-8 sentence texts | concrete + simple descriptive words | light: prompts/checklist | describe + give a simple reason |
| 10 | Bridge | Weather and Seasons | simple present + connector + "I like it better" (chunk, not productive comparative) | 6-8 sentence texts | concrete + simple preference chunk | light | describe + state a simple preference |
| 11 | Bridge | Social Media and Tech | simple present + connector + frequency words combined | 6-8 sentence texts | concrete + simple habit words | light | describe habits + give a simple reason |
| 12 | Bridge | Weekend Plans | simple present + "want to" (simple forward-looking chunk, not "going to" future) | 6-8 sentence texts | concrete + simple plan words | light | describe + state a simple plan |

Review lessons (Lesson 5 of every unit) stay tier-matched: Unit 4's
review is foundation-level, Unit 8's is combination-level, Unit 12's is
bridge-level.

## Lesson-level escalation within each unit

Every unit's 5 lessons follow the same internal shape, regardless of
tier — only the absolute difficulty shifts with the tier above:

| Lesson | Role | Scaffolding | Task independence |
|--------|------|--------------|--------------------|
| 1 | Notice / receptive | Heaviest in the unit | Matching, identifying, single-word answers |
| 2 | Controlled practice | Full frames still present | Short-phrase answers, guided |
| 3 | Freer practice | Partial frames | Fuller sentence answers, less copying |
| 4 | Production | Lightest in the unit | Combine Lessons 1-3's language into new output (speaking/writing) |
| 5 | Review | Matches the unit's tier | Synthesize the whole unit |

## Task-format rotation (per the 25-min 1:1 micro-pattern doc)

The 9-slide architecture doesn't change, but the *task format* inside
`activity` and `speaking` rotates lesson-to-lesson within a unit, so
lessons don't feel like copies of each other even at the same
difficulty:

| Lesson slot | Activity-slide format | Speaking-slide format |
|-------------|------------------------|--------------------------|
| Lesson 1 | Matching / identify-the-target-word | Teacher models, student echoes/completes |
| Lesson 2 | Fill-in-the-blank with word bank | Guided Q&A exchange |
| Lesson 3 | Choose-the-better-answer / error-spot | Student asks, teacher answers (role reversal) |
| Lesson 4 | Reorder or complete-the-response | Mini role-play or one-minute talk |
| Lesson 5 | Mixed review (one item from each earlier format) | Free Q&A combining the whole unit |

This rotation repeats every unit, so teachers always know lesson 1 of
any unit uses a matching-style activity, lesson 4 uses role-play, etc.
— consistent enough to train against, varied enough not to feel like
the same lesson with new words.
