# A1 Adults — Intra-Level Progression Matrix (Pilot #4)

Per the Sentivo Intra-Level Progression Framework, adapted for Adults'
**steepest slope** (per the framework's age-track guidance: less
scaffolding sooner, earlier integration of realistic functional texts,
faster move to independent production than Kids or Teens).

**Scope note:** none of A1 Adults is live in Supabase yet (all 12 units
are still unrun SQL), so this pass rebuilds all 12 units directly,
unlike A1 Kids where Units 1-2 stayed untouched.

**Ceiling guardrail:** same A1 boundary as Kids/Teens (no comparatives,
no simple past narrative, no "going to" future) -- that line is set by
CEFR level, not age track. What differs for Adults is how quickly
scaffolding drops and how "adult-functional" the texts get: forms,
short messages, appointment requests, and shop/workplace transactions
appear from the Combination tier onward, not saved for later levels.
Adults can also handle a single-clause "because" reason from the
Combination tier onward (Kids/Teens hold that back to Bridge or avoid
it), since that's ordinary A1 functional register for adult learners,
not abstract reasoning.

## Unit-level tiers

| Unit | Tier | Topic | Grammar load | Text length target | Vocabulary type | Scaffolding | Cognitive demand |
|------|------|-------|---------------|---------------------|------------------|--------------|-------------------|
| 1 | Foundation | Hello and Introductions | "to be" + simple present, basic Wh-questions | 3-5 sentence texts | concrete, personal info | heavy: sentence frames | name / identify |
| 2 | Foundation | My Family | simple present + possessives | 3-5 sentence texts | concrete (family nouns) | heavy | name / identify |
| 3 | Foundation | Home and Everyday Objects | simple present + "there is/are" + location | 3-5 sentence texts | concrete (home nouns) | heavy | name / identify / locate |
| 4 | Foundation | Daily Routine | simple present + basic time expressions | 3-5 sentence texts | concrete (routine words) | heavy | describe a routine |
| 5 | Combination | Work and Jobs | simple present + negative + question, same exchange | 5-7 sentence texts | concrete + workplace words | medium: partial prompts, short model texts | describe + simple reason ("because") |
| 6 | Combination | Shopping and Money | simple present + "I'd like" + quantities | 5-7 sentence texts | concrete + numbers/prices | medium, realistic shop dialogue | make a simple request |
| 7 | Combination | Food and Drinks | simple present + like/don't like + "because" | 5-7 sentence texts | concrete + simple opinion words | medium | describe + give a one-clause reason |
| 8 | Combination | Technology and Communication | simple present + frequency words + question | 5-7 sentence texts | concrete + habit words | medium | describe habits + give a reason |
| 9 | Bridge | Travel and Transport | simple present + connector ("and"/"but") combined | 6-8 sentence texts | concrete + travel/functional words | light: prompts/checklist, real ticket/schedule texts | describe + make a simple request |
| 10 | Bridge | Health and Feelings | simple present + "because" + simple advice chunk ("You should...") | 6-8 sentence texts | concrete + feelings/health words | light | describe a problem + give/receive simple advice |
| 11 | Bridge | Places in Town | simple present + connector + location/direction language | 6-8 sentence texts | concrete + direction words | light | describe + give simple directions |
| 12 | Bridge | Appointments and Future Plans | simple present + "I'd like to" / "I want to" (chunk, not "going to") | 6-8 sentence texts | concrete + planning words | light | describe + make/respond to a simple plan |

Review lessons (Lesson 5 of every unit) stay tier-matched: Unit 4's
review is foundation-level, Unit 8's is combination-level, Unit 12's is
bridge-level.

## Lesson-level escalation within each unit

Same shape as A1 Kids/Teens, but built around realistic adult texts
(short forms, messages, shop/workplace dialogues) instead of
picture-matching or TPR:

| Lesson | Role | Scaffolding | Task independence |
|--------|------|--------------|--------------------|
| 1 | Notice / receptive | Heaviest: read a short model text and identify | Matching, identifying, short answers |
| 2 | Controlled practice | Full frames still present | Fill in a form/dialogue, guided |
| 3 | Freer practice | Partial frames, choose-the-better-response | Fuller sentence answers, role reversal |
| 4 | Production | Lightest in the unit | Mini role-play or short written message combining Lessons 1-3 |
| 5 | Review | Matches the unit's tier | Synthesize the whole unit |

## Task-format rotation

| Lesson slot | Activity-slide format | Speaking-slide format |
|-------------|------------------------|--------------------------|
| Lesson 1 | Read a short model text, identify the target phrase | Teacher models, student echoes/completes |
| Lesson 2 | Fill-in-the-blank with word bank | Guided Q&A exchange |
| Lesson 3 | Choose-the-better-response / error-spot | Student asks, teacher answers (role reversal) |
| Lesson 4 | Complete-the-response / reorder a short exchange | Mini role-play (shop, workplace, appointment) |
| Lesson 5 | Mixed review (one item from each earlier format) | Free Q&A combining the whole unit |
