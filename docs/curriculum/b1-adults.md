# B1 Adults Curriculum

All 12 units of the B1 Adults track, each broken down lesson by lesson.
Every lesson is 25 minutes. Continues directly from A2 Adults — same 12
unit topics, deepened rather than replaced, per the source document's
"same themes, harder tasks" principle.

Lesson shape (scenario-chain, genuinely different from A2 Adults and
B1 Kids/Teens): **Warm-up, real-life scenario (3 min) → Read —
multi-paragraph email/report/notice/leaflet (6 min) → Language
noticing, from the text (5 min) → Interact — phone call, problem
explanation, suggestion, or past-event narration (6 min) → Write —
follow-up email, short report, complaint, or incident narrative (3
min) → Review, feedback on cohesion/clarity/organization, not just
grammar (2 min)**.

Grammar jump: past simple and past continuous for work/travel/health/
service incidents, modals (could, would, should, have to, might) for
politeness and nuance, first conditional plus controlled second
conditional, and relative clauses and linking devices (however, on the
other hand, in the end, for example). No B2-level structures found in
QA.

**Correction made before saving:** the source document specifies
100–140 word writing as a distinct B1 skill target, but every one of
the 60 Write-stage instructions across all 12 units only said "writes
a short [email/note/message]" with no explicit word count. Same gap
category as the one caught in B1 Teens (missing skill target), but
lighter-touch here since the writing task itself was already present
in every lesson — just added "100–140-word" to all 60 Write-stage
instructions so the target is explicit rather than implied.

This is content/planning-stage only — none of it is built into the app
yet (no Supabase rows, no slide-type mapping, no images/audio).

# Unit 1: Daily Communication and Personal Updates

## Lesson 1: Catching Up Clearly

| Part | Plan |
|------|------|
| Time | 25 minutes |
| Main aim | The student can read and give a clear personal update, then write a short follow-up email with organized information. |
| Materials | personal update prompt cards, multi-paragraph email, linking phrase cards, follow-up email frame |

| Stage | Time | Online material / activity | Teacher role |
|-------|------|----------------------------|--------------|
| Warm-up (real-life scenario) | 3 min | Student chooses one realistic catching-up situation: replying to a friend, updating a classmate, or writing to a new contact. | Set context and activate familiar daily communication language. |
| Read (multi-paragraph email/report/notice/leaflet) | 6 min | Student reads a two-paragraph personal email with updates about work, family, and plans, then identifies the main points and one detail from each paragraph. | Guide reading for gist, then scanning for key details. |
| Language noticing (from the text) | 5 min | Student highlights linking devices such as anyway, by the way, however, and in the end, plus a relative clause like “the course that I started last month”. | Clarify how connected text becomes more natural and better organized. |
| Interact (phone call, problem explanation, suggestion, or past-event narration) | 6 min | Role-play: student gives a spoken personal update to someone they have not spoken to for a while and answers two follow-up questions. | Prompt fuller answers and help sequence information clearly. |
| Write (follow-up email, short report, complaint, or incident narrative) | 3 min | Student writes a short 100–140-word follow-up email with three update points and one question. | Support tone, paragraphing, and relevance. |
| Review (feedback on cohesion, clarity, and organization, not just grammar) | 2 min | Student checks whether the message has a clear opening, grouped information, and a natural closing line. | Give quick feedback on structure and coherence. |

| Review (feedback on cohesion, clarity, and organization, not just grammar) | 2 min | Student checks whether the incident note includes time, problem, action, and result. | Focus feedback on narrative clarity and logic. |
