# FORGE — Track 1 Full Draft (Lessons 1–10)

**Track:** "A Day at Work" · Software Engineer · Tech · Level A2
**Purpose of this document:** a complete, populated reference draft of one full FORGE track (10 lessons), meant to be fed to another model (alongside the four format prompts below) so it can generate new tracks for other occupations/interests using this exact pattern.

---

## How a FORGE track works

- A **track** = one learner profile (occupation + interest + level) told across **10 lessons**.
- Every track follows the **same 4-format sequence**, in this fixed order regardless of the learner's occupation:
  - **L1–L2 — Say the Picture** (diagnostic/baseline, no homework check, image-driven)
  - **L3–L4 — Fill the Gap** (past simple → going to + time)
  - **L5–L7 — Echoing** (recycled mix → prefer/rather → habits)
  - **L8–L10 — Question Chain** (travel → tech → problem-solving; L10 is the final lesson)
- **L2 always reuses L1's content exactly** — same words, same everything, just relabeled "L2." It exists so the learner gets two passes at the diagnostic format before the track moves on.
- Each lesson drills **5 target words**, chosen to fit the lesson's topic and the occupation/interest the track is built around.
- **Homework chains forward:** a lesson's "next lesson's 5 words" always match the *next* lesson's actual Word Load — the learner previews next class's vocabulary via homework before the lesson even starts. The following lesson's "Homework Check" slide then just re-displays that same lesson's own words (no external lookup needed).
- **L10 is always the final lesson**: no new homework words ("review your favourite words from all 10 lessons" instead), and the Scorecard gets one extra line comparing today's score to the Lesson 1 baseline.

## What changes per track vs. what stays fixed

| Stays fixed across every track | Changes per track |
|---|---|
| The 4 techniques and their L1–L10 order | Occupation, interest, level |
| The slide skeleton per format (see prompts below) | Every lesson's title, topic, and 5 words |
| Scorecard rows (Range/3, Accuracy/3, Fluency/6, Interaction/6, Total/18) | Word images (`imageNote`), frames, questions, follow-ups, examples |
| Escape hatches ("Describe it / Near enough / Buy time") on Gap/Echo/Chain lessons | Warm-up questions, Push It prompts+models, can-do lines |
| L1/L2 have no escape hatches, no Homework Check, no voice-note homework (diagnostic lessons) | The homework passage (L1/L2 only) |

## Lesson-by-lesson content

### L1 & L2 — "A Day at Work" · Say the Picture · Baseline

*(L2 is an exact duplicate of L1 — same content, relabeled "L2.")*

**Subtitle:** Talk about your day — morning to evening.
**Technique line:** Say the Picture — look, then talk.

**5 words:**

| Word | Picture | Sentence starter | Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| arrive | Someone arriving at work in the morning, walking in the door with a coffee. | "In the morning, I arrive…" | to get to a place | She arrives at the office at 9 a.m. |
| review | Someone looking closely at a computer screen, reading through something. | "First, I review…" | to look at something carefully | I review my messages every morning. |
| solve | Someone fixing a problem or debugging code, focused and thinking. | "When something breaks, I solve…" | to find the answer to a problem | She solved the bug in under an hour. |
| develop | A small team working together around a laptop on a project. | "With my team, I develop…" | to build or create something over time | Our team develops a new feature every month. |
| wrap up | Someone leaving work in the evening, a wall clock showing 5 o'clock. | "At the end of the day, I wrap up…" | to finish something | Let's wrap up the meeting in five minutes. |

**Slides:**
1. **Cover** — Title "A Day at Work," subtitle, 5 word chips, technique line.
2. **Warm-up** — Heading "Say it out loud." Questions: "What's your job?" / "Where do you work?" / "What time do you arrive?"
3. **Word Load** — 5 big tappable word tokens (no pictures shown here); tapping a word zooms into a card with the word, its meaning, and its example sentence.
4. **Say the Picture ×5** — one slide per word: a big centered picture, the word, and its starter underneath.
5. **Your Turn** (diagnostic) — Prompt: "Walk me through your day — from arrive to wrap up. (3–4 sentences)." Guide-rail list of all 5 starters shown as a scaffold.
6. **Push It** (optional) — Prompt: "Which part of your day do you like most — and why?" Model frame: "I like… because…"
7. **Scorecard** — Can-do line: "I can describe my workday using: arrive, review, solve, develop, wrap up." Rows: Range /3, Accuracy /3, Fluency /6, Interaction /6, Total /18. Tag: "baseline."
8. **Homework** — Heading "Grow your words before next class." A short passage (Maya's workday) with the 5 words highlighted inline; tapping a highlighted word shows its meaning + example. *(This is the only homework style that uses a passage instead of a 3-item list — specific to L1/L2's diagnostic format.)*

### L3 — "Past Experiences" · Fill the Gap · Progress

**Grammar focus:** past simple.
**Subtitle:** Talk about something you did recently.
**Technique line:** Fill the Gap — I give you the start, you finish it out loud.

**5 words:**

| Word | Picture | Frame (with blank) | Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| visited | Someone standing in front of a famous landmark, looking amazed, camera in hand. | "Last month, I visited ______." | went to see a place | She visited Lisbon last summer. |
| tried | Someone tasting new food for the first time, curious expression. | "For the first time, I tried ______." | did or tasted something new | I tried sushi for the first time last week. |
| met | Two people shaking hands and smiling, meeting for the first time. | "Last week, I met ______." | saw and talked to someone for the first time | I met my new neighbor yesterday. |
| stayed | Someone checking into a hotel room, suitcase beside them. | "On my trip, I stayed ______." | lived somewhere for a short time | We stayed at a small hotel near the beach. |
| lost | Someone searching through their bag, looking worried. | "One time, I lost ______." | could not find something anymore | I lost my keys on the train once. |

**Slides:**
1. **Cover**
2. **Warm-up** — "What did you do last weekend?" / "When was the last time you tried something new?"
3. **Homework Check** — "Words you grew at home," shows the 5 words above as chips, line: "Tell me one thing you recorded."
4. **Word Load** — clean list: each row = word + small picture + frame-with-blank.
5. **How It Works** — line: "Read the start → fill the blank → say the whole sentence." Demo: "Last month, I visited ______." → "Last month, I visited Lisbon."
6. **Gap Sprint** (game) — internal 1-of-5 stepper: big picture + frame-with-blank shown one at a time, "Finish each sentence out loud, then go to the next," escape hatches (Describe it / Near enough / Buy time) visible throughout.
7. **Your Turn** — Prompt: "Tell me about something you did recently. (3–4 sentences)." Guide-rail of the 5 frames. Escape hatches.
8. **Push It** — Prompt: "Tell me about two things that happened on the same day — join them with because, but, or so." Model: "I visited the museum, but it was closed, so I went to the park instead."
9. **Scorecard** — Can-do line: "I can talk about things I did recently using: visited, tried, met, stayed, lost." Tag: "progress."
10. **Homework** (3-item list) — Read your passage (placeholder) / Next lesson's 5 words: "meet up · book · cancel · plan · join" / Voice note: "Talk about a plan you have. Use each new word once."

### L4 — "Making Plans" · Fill the Gap · Progress

**Grammar focus:** going to + time.
**Subtitle:** Talk about your plans.

**5 words:**

| Word | Picture | Frame | Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| meet up | Two friends texting or talking about weekend plans, smiling at their phones. | "This weekend, I'm going to meet up with ______." | to see someone you arranged to see | I'm meeting up with my sister on Saturday. |
| book | Someone booking tickets on a laptop, confirmation screen visible. | "First, I'm going to book ______." | to arrange or reserve something in advance | I need to book a table for dinner. |
| cancel | Someone crossing out an event on a wall calendar. | "If it rains, I'll cancel ______." | to say an event will not happen | We had to cancel the picnic because of the storm. |
| plan | Someone writing in a planner or looking at a calendar, thinking. | "Next, I'm going to plan ______." | to decide what to do before doing it | I'm planning a trip for next month. |
| join | Someone waving, joining a group of people already gathered. | "I'm going to ask ______ to join." | to take part in something with other people | Do you want to join us for lunch? |

**Slides:** same 10-slide skeleton as L3.
- **Warm-up:** "What are you going to do this weekend?" / "Do you have any plans for next month?"
- **How It Works demo:** "This weekend, I'm going to meet up with ______." → "…with my sister."
- **Your Turn prompt:** "Tell me about a plan you have coming up. (3–4 sentences)"
- **Push It:** "Tell me about a backup plan — what will you do if your first plan doesn't work?" Model: "I'm going to book a table outside, but if it rains, I'll cancel it and go to a restaurant instead."
- **Scorecard can-do:** "I can talk about my plans using: meet up, book, cancel, plan, join."
- **Homework next words:** "order · visited · meet up · book · plan" (these are recycled words feeding L5's mix). Voice note: "Describe your plan for the weekend, step by step."

### L5 — "Mix: Errands & Plans" · Echoing · Progress

**Recycles words from L1–L4.**
**Subtitle:** Talk about your errands and plans.
**Technique line:** Echoing — start your answer with my words, then keep going.

**5 words (Q → echoed A):**

| Word | Question | Answer frame | Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| order | "What do you usually order?" | "I usually order ______, because ______." | to ask for food or something to be brought to you | I usually order pizza, because it's quick. |
| visited | "Where did you visit recently?" | "I visited ______ last ______." | went to see a place | I visited Lisbon last spring. |
| meet up | "Who do you meet up with?" | "I meet up with ______ on ______." | to see someone you arranged to see | I meet up with my sister on Sundays. |
| book | "What do you book online?" | "I book ______ when ______." | to arrange or reserve something in advance | I book tickets online when I plan a trip. |
| plan | "What are you planning this week?" | "This week, I'm planning to ______." | to decide what to do before doing it | This week, I'm planning to clean the apartment. |

**Slides (10, no pictures — this format is question/answer only, no images):**
1. Cover
2. Warm-up — "What do you usually do after work?" / "What errands do you have this week?"
3. Homework Check — "Tell me one thing you recorded."
4. Word Load — list of word + Q → A frame (no image column).
5. How It Works — "Hear the question → repeat the key words → finish the sentence." Demo: Q "What do you usually order?" → A "I usually order pizza, because it's quick."
6. Echo Round (game) — 1-of-5 stepper showing one question at a time, no picture. Instruction: "Repeat my words to start, then answer in a full sentence." Escape hatches.
7. Your Turn — Prompt: "I'll ask about your week — errands and plans. Echo my words, then keep talking." *(No guide-rail list for Echoing's Your Turn — just the prompt + escape hatches.)*
8. Push It — "Echo one of my questions, answer it, then add one more sentence I didn't ask for." Model: "I usually order pizza, because it's quick. I also like it because I can share it with friends."
9. Scorecard — "I can answer questions about errands and plans using: order, visited, meet up, book, plan."
10. Homework — Next words: "prefer · enjoy · mind · rather · depends." Voice note: "Answer 3 questions about what you like. Start each answer by repeating my question words."

### L6 — "Opinions & Preferences" · Echoing · Progress

**Grammar focus:** prefer / would rather + reasons.
**Subtitle:** Talk about your opinions and preferences.

**5 words:**

| Word | Question | Answer frame | Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| prefer | "Where do you prefer to work?" | "I prefer to work ______, because ______." | to like one thing more than another | I prefer to work from home, because it's quiet. |
| enjoy | "What do you enjoy on weekends?" | "I enjoy ______ on weekends." | to get pleasure from doing something | I enjoy hiking on weekends. |
| mind | "Do you mind working late?" | "I don't mind ______, but ______." | to feel annoyed or bothered by something | I don't mind working late, but I need a break first. |
| rather | "Would you rather stay in or go out?" | "I'd rather ______ than ______." | to prefer one choice over another | I'd rather stay in than go out tonight. |
| depends | "Do you like your job?" | "It depends — ______." | the answer changes based on something else | It depends — some days are better than others. |

**Slides:** same skeleton as L5.
- **Warm-up:** "Do you prefer mornings or evenings?" / "What's something you really enjoy doing?"
- **How It Works demo:** Q "Where do you prefer to work?" → A "I prefer to work from home, because it's quiet."
- **Your Turn prompt:** "I'll ask about your opinions. Echo my words, then give a reason each time."
- **Push It:** "Echo one question, answer it, then add one more sentence I didn't ask for." Model: "I prefer to work from home, because it's quiet. I also get more done without interruptions."
- **Scorecard can-do:** "I can talk about my opinions using: prefer, enjoy, mind, rather, depends."
- **Homework next words:** "cut down · keep up · skip · stick to · quit." Voice note: "Answer 3 questions about your habits. Echo each question, then add a reason."

### L7 — "Health & Habits" · Echoing · Progress

**Grammar focus:** present simple habits + frequency.
**Subtitle:** Talk about your health and habits.

**5 words:**

| Word | Question | Answer frame | Meaning | Example |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| cut down | "What do you want to cut down on?" | "I want to cut down on ______." | to reduce how much of something you do or have | I want to cut down on coffee. |
| keep up | "What habit do you keep up?" | "I keep up ______ every ______." | to continue doing something regularly | I keep up running every week. |
| skip | "Do you ever skip meals?" | "I sometimes skip ______, when ______." | to not do something you usually do | I sometimes skip breakfast, when I'm in a hurry. |
| stick to | "What routine do you stick to?" | "I stick to ______ because ______." | to continue following a plan or routine | I stick to a morning routine because it keeps me focused. |
| quit | "Is there anything you want to quit?" | "I want to quit ______." | to stop doing something completely | I want to quit checking my phone before bed. |

**Slides:** same skeleton.
- **Warm-up:** "How often do you exercise?" / "What's one healthy habit you have?"
- **How It Works demo:** Q "What do you want to cut down on?" → A "I want to cut down on coffee."
- **Your Turn prompt:** "I'll ask about your health and habits. Echo my words, then keep the answer going."
- **Push It:** Model: "I want to cut down on coffee. I usually have three cups a day, so I'm trying to stop after lunch."
- **Scorecard can-do:** "I can talk about my habits using: cut down, keep up, skip, stick to, quit."
- **Homework next words:** "head to · get off · transfer · book · miss" (feeds L8, first Question Chain lesson). Voice note: "Answer 3 questions about your daily habits. Echo each question, then add one extra sentence on your own."

### L8 — "Travel & Directions" · Question Chain · Progress

**Grammar focus:** prepositions of movement.
**Subtitle:** Talk about how you travel.
**Technique line:** Question Chain — one question from me. You answer it, and the two I didn't ask yet.

**How the technique works (used verbatim as the demo on every Question Chain lesson):**
> "1 question → (silently think 2 follow-ups) → 1 folded answer."
> Demo Q: "What did you do this weekend?" → silent follow-ups (Who with? / Why did you like it?) → folded A: "I went hiking. I went with my brother because he loves the mountains, and I liked it because the weather was perfect."

**5 words:**

| Word | Single question | Follow-ups (silent) | Folded example answer | Meaning |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| head to | "How do you usually get to work?" | Where? / When? | "I usually head to the station in the morning, because it's near my office." | to go toward a place |
| get off | "Where do you usually get off?" | Which stop? / Why? | "I get off at the third stop, because it's closest to work." | to leave a bus, train, or other vehicle |
| transfer | "Do you have to transfer on your way to work?" | Where? / How often? | "I transfer at the main station every day, and it takes ten minutes." | to change from one bus or train to another |
| book | "How do you book your travel tickets?" | What? / When? | "I book my tickets online the night before, so I don't wait in line." | to arrange or reserve something in advance |
| miss | "Have you ever missed a train or bus?" | What happened? / What next? | "Once I missed my train, so I had to wait, but I still arrived on time." | to not catch a bus, train, or flight in time |

**Slides (10):**
1. Cover
2. Warm-up — "How do you usually get to work?" / "Do you like traveling by train or car?"
3. Homework Check — line here is different from Gap/Echo: **"Tell me the whole story."**
4. Word Load — list: word + follow-ups + folded example (no image).
5. How It Works — the demo above.
6. Chain It (game) — 1-of-5 stepper, one single question shown at a time + its 2 follow-ups as a silent hint ("Think: Where? · When?"). Instruction: "I ask ONE question and stop. You answer it — and the two questions I didn't ask. Join your sentences with because, and, so, or but." Escape hatches.
7. Your Turn — Prompt: "Tell me about how you travel to work or on a trip." *(No guide-rail.)* Extra note shown: "Before you speak, think — what two things would I naturally ask next? Answer those too." Escape hatches.
8. Push It — "Chain three follow-ups this time, or tell a 5–6 sentence mini-story from one prompt." Model: "I traveled to Rome last spring. I went with two friends because we wanted a short break, we stayed near the center so we could walk everywhere, and we loved it so much that we're already planning to go back."
9. Scorecard — "I can tell a connected story about travel using: head to, get off, transfer, book, miss."
10. Homework — Next words: "download · update · back up · log in · crash." Voice note: "Answer one question about a recent trip. Fold in two follow-ups."

### L9 — "Technology in Daily Life" · Question Chain · Progress

**Grammar focus:** present perfect (have you ever…).
**Subtitle:** Talk about technology in your daily life.

**5 words:**

| Word | Single question | Follow-ups | Folded example answer | Meaning |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| download | "Have you downloaded any apps recently?" | What? / Why? | "I've downloaded a few apps this week, because I needed them for work." | to copy a file or program from the internet to your device |
| update | "When did you last update your phone?" | When? / What happened? | "I updated my phone last night, and now it works faster." | to change something to its newest version |
| back up | "Do you back up your files?" | How often? / Why? | "I back up my files every week, so I never lose anything important." | to make a copy of files in case the original is lost |
| log in | "How often do you log in to your work system?" | Where? / Any problem? | "I log in to the system every morning, but sometimes it's slow." | to enter your details to access an account or system |
| crash | "Has your computer ever crashed on you?" | When? / What did you do? | "My laptop crashed yesterday, so I restarted it, and then it was fine." | when a device or program suddenly stops working |

**Slides:** same skeleton as L8; **How It Works reuses the identical hiking demo** (the demo illustrates the *technique*, not lesson content, so it stays constant across all Question Chain lessons).
- **Warm-up:** "Have you ever had a problem with your phone?" / "What apps do you use every day?"
- **Your Turn prompt:** "Tell me about a time technology helped you or gave you a problem."
- **Push It model:** "My phone crashed during an important call last month. I panicked because I was talking to a client, so I called back from my laptop instead, and luckily they understood."
- **Scorecard can-do:** "I can tell a connected story about technology using: download, update, back up, log in, crash."
- **Homework next words:** "complain · refund · replace · fix · sort out." Voice note: "Answer one question about your tech habits. Fold in two follow-ups."

### L10 — "Solving a Small Problem" · Question Chain · **FINAL LESSON**

**Grammar focus:** polite complaint forms.
**Subtitle:** Talk about solving a problem — your final lesson.

**5 words:**

| Word | Single question | Follow-ups | Folded example answer | Meaning |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| complain | "Have you ever complained about something?" | About what? / To whom? | "I complained about a late delivery, and I spoke to the manager politely." | to say you are unhappy about something |
| refund | "Have you ever asked for a refund?" | Why? / Did you get it? | "I asked for a refund because the item was broken, and they agreed." | money given back for something returned |
| replace | "Have you ever had something replaced?" | What? / How? | "They offered to replace it, so I sent the old one back." | to give a new one in place of a broken or lost one |
| fix | "Have you ever had something fixed quickly?" | What? / How long? | "They fixed the problem in two days, which was faster than I expected." | to repair something that's broken |
| sort out | "Have you ever had to sort out a problem?" | How? / Feeling? | "In the end, we sorted it out, and I was happy with the result." | to solve a problem or organize something |

**Slides:** same skeleton, with two final-lesson differences:
- **Warm-up:** "Have you ever had to complain about something?" / "How do you usually solve a problem calmly?"
- **Your Turn prompt:** "Tell me about a time you had to solve a problem or complain about something."
- **Push It model:** "I complained about a broken order last year. I called the company because the item arrived damaged, they offered a refund right away, and I was happy with how quickly they sorted it out."
- **Scorecard** — can-do: "I can tell a connected story about solving a problem using: complain, refund, replace, fix, sort out." Tag: **"final"** (not "progress"). **Extra line below the score rows:** "Compare this score to your baseline from Lesson 1 — how far have you come?"
- **Homework** — heading is the same, but item 2 changes: **"Review your words" / "No new words this time — review your favourite words from all 10 lessons."** Voice note: "Answer one question with a full chained turn — this is your final recording to show how far you've come."

## Notes for generating a new track

1. Pick a new occupation + interest + level (e.g., "Nurse · Healthcare · A2" or "Barista · Hospitality · A2").
2. Keep the 4-format sequence and 10-lesson skeleton exactly as shown above — only the *content* changes, never the structure.
3. Write a track title and blurb parallel to "A Day at Work" / "10 lessons built around your job and your world..."
4. For each lesson, invent a topic that fits both the fixed technique for that lesson number *and* the learner's occupation/interest (e.g., a nurse's L3 "Past Experiences" might be about a memorable shift instead of a trip).
5. Author 5 words per lesson with the correct shape for that lesson's format (`imageNote`+`frame` for Say the Picture/Fill the Gap, `question`+`answer` for Echoing, `question`+`followups`+`example` for Question Chain) plus `meaning`+`example` for every word (used by the Word Load zoom card).
6. Chain homework "next words" forward exactly as shown — each lesson's next-words list must equal the following lesson's actual 5 words.
7. Reuse the fixed Question Chain demo text as-is (it teaches the technique, not the topic) unless you want a topic-flavored demo instead — either works, L8–L10 above use the identical demo throughout.
8. L2 = duplicate L1 exactly, relabeled. L10 gets the compareLine + no-new-words homework treatment.
